Research Proposal

Exploring the lived experiences towards dynamic disability among individuals with Ehlers-Danlos Syndrome

Researcher: Natanya Khanna Guide: Dr. Patrick Jude L.

Ehlers‚ÄìDanlos Syndrome (EDS) is a rare, inherited connective tissue disorder characterized by multisystemic issues such as chronic pain, fatigue, and fluctuating symptom severity. Despite medical research on its physiology and genetics, the unique psychosocial challenges remain underexplored, particularly from the patient's perspective.

This study aims to explore how individuals diagnosed with EDS perceive dynamic disability and how such perceptions shape their emotional wellbeing, resilience, and everyday functioning. It addresses the conflict between physical capability and willingness, illness uncertainty, and the "invisible" nature of the condition.

Adopting a qualitative phenomenological design, the research involves semi-structured interviews interpreted through Leventhal‚Äôs Common Sense Model of Self-Regulation. The goal is to develop a psychosocial model that integrates emotional and social dimensions into healthcare settings, fostering more holistic, empathetic, and patient-centered approaches.
